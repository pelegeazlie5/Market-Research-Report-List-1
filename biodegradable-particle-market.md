<p><p>The biodegradable particle market primarily consists of PLA (Polylactic Acid) and PLGA (Poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid)) particles. PLA particles are derived from renewable resources like corn starch, offering excellent biocompatibility and mechanical strength, making them suitable for various applications in medical devices and packaging. PLGA particles combine lactic and glycolic acids, allowing for tailored degradation rates and controlled drug release, which is advantageous in pharmaceuticals and tissue engineering. Both types are gaining traction due to their eco-friendly properties and versatility.</p></p>

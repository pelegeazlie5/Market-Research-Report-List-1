<p><p>The composite geomembrane market is categorized into several types based on the materials used. High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) geomembranes offer excellent chemical resistance and durability, making them suitable for environmental containment. Linear Low-Density Polyethylene (LLDPE) provides enhanced flexibility and weldability. Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) geomembranes are known for their versatility and ease of installation. Flexible Polypropylene (FPP) exhibits superior flexibility and resistance to UV and chemicals. Additionally, other materials may include reinforced fabrics or specialized compounds tailored for specific applications.</p></p>

<p><p>Homeopathic dilutions, particularly 6C and 30C, indicate the level of dilution and succussion (vigorous shaking) a remedy has undergone. The "C" stands for "centesimal," meaning each dilution step involves mixing one part of the substance with 99 parts of diluent, repeated for the specified number of times. 6C implies six rounds of this process, while 30C denotes thirty rounds. These market types cater to varying patient sensitivities and conditions, with higher dilutions often perceived as more potent in homeopathic practice.</p></p>

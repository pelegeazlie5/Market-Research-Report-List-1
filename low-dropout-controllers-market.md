<p><p>Low Dropout Controllers (LDOs) are essential for voltage regulation in various electronic devices. The market is primarily divided into two types: adjustable and fixed. Adjustable LDOs allow users to set the output voltage by configuring external resistors, offering flexibility for diverse applications. In contrast, fixed LDOs provide a predetermined output voltage, simplifying design and implementation for specific needs. Both types cater to different user requirements, with adjustable models favored for versatility, while fixed models are preferred for ease of use.</p></p>
